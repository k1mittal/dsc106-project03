Rationale for Design Decisions

Choice of Visual Encoding

We wanted to reveal any relationship between physiological measures and exam performance, so plotting each student’s score against their EDA (or whichever metric/statistic is selected) made correlation patterns immediately visible.

  • Color for Student Identity: Unique hues for each of the 10 students let viewers trace one student’s performance across Midterm 1, Midterm 2, and Final.
  • Shape for Exam Type: Circles, squares, and triangles encode Midterm 1, Midterm 2, and Final respectively: so differences by exam are instantly clear.
  • Opacity & Size: Moderately opaque, uniform markers prevent overlap from hiding data yet avoid visual dominance by any single point.
  • Dropdown Controls: “Measure” (EDA, Heart Rate, etc.) and “Statistic” (Average, Max, Min) selectors allow on-the-fly comparisons without reloading.
  • Tooltips & Legend Placement: Hover tooltips reveal student ID, exact values, and scores; the legend on the right stays out of the plot area but remains accessible.

Alternatives Considered

  • Small multiples (one chart per exam): discarded because cross-exam comparisons become less immediate.
  • Using color alone for both student and exam: rejected to avoid confusion and improve accessibility.
  • Brushing & linked detail panel: scoped as a stretch goal, deferred to keep the interface lean.
